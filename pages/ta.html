Project Overview

Digitalizing thesis administration with an integrated web platform.

Developed a web-based Thesis Registration Information System to replace manual administrative processes using Google Forms. The platform centralizes thesis registration, supervision records, seminar applications, and administrative workflows, enabling students, lecturers, and administrators to collaborate through a single integrated system while improving operational efficiency.

My Role
Fullstack Web Developer

Responsible for developing frontend and backend modules, database design, authentication, and business logic using Laravel.

Duration
2025 - 2026

Undergraduate Thesis Project at Universitas Singaperbangsa Karawang.

Problem Statement

Challenges Before the System Was Developed

The thesis registration process relied on Google Forms and separate digital documents, making administration inefficient and difficult to monitor throughout each stage of the thesis lifecycle.

Manual Document Processing

Thesis registration required multiple manual documents, increasing administrative effort and processing time.

Limited Progress Monitoring

Students, lecturers, and administrators had difficulty tracking thesis progress and approval status.

Unstructured Supervision Records

Supervision activities and feedback were not centrally documented, making academic progress difficult to review.

Proposed Solution

Centralized Thesis Registration Platform

Developed an integrated web-based information system that digitalizes the entire thesis administration process within a single platform.

Online Registration

Allows students to submit thesis registrations and seminar applications digitally.

Thesis Progress Monitoring

Provides real-time monitoring of registration status, supervision progress, and approval workflows.

Supervision Management

Centralizes supervision records and lecturer feedback for easier documentation and communication.

System Design

System Architecture

The application follows a layered architecture where users interact with the web interface, requests are processed by Laravel, business logic is executed, and data is stored in MySQL.

Database Design

Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)

The database was designed using a relational model to ensure data consistency, reduce redundancy, and support role-based workflows for students, lecturers, and administrators.

The ERD illustrates relationships between users, students, lecturers, proposals, supervisors, schedules, seminar registrations, and thesis administration.

Database Structure

Main Database Tables

Users

Stores authentication credentials, roles, and account information.

Students

Student academic profile and thesis registration data.

Lecturers

Supervisor and examiner information.

Proposal

Seminar

Seminar registration and scheduling data.

Schedules

Seminar timetable, room, and examiner assignments.

Security

Authentication Flow

The application uses Laravel's built-in authentication with role-based authorization to ensure secure access for students, lecturers, and administrators. After successful login, users are authenticated and redirected to their respective dashboards based on assigned roles.
